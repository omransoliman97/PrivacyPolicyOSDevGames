# Privacy Policy for Block OS

Effective date: May 7, 2026

Block OS is a casual puzzle game published under the package name
`com.osdev.games_blockos`.

## Information We Collect

Block OS does not require an account and does not ask players to provide a name,
email address, phone number, or other direct personal information.

The app may use third-party services that collect app, device, advertising, and
usage data to provide analytics, notifications, and ads:

- Google Firebase Analytics
- Firebase Cloud Messaging
- Google AdMob

These services may process identifiers such as device information, advertising
IDs, app interaction events, crash or diagnostic information, notification
tokens, and approximate location inferred by the service provider.

## How Information Is Used

Information may be used to:

- Understand gameplay and improve Block OS
- Show and measure ads, including rewarded ads
- Support push notifications
- Diagnose technical issues and improve stability
- Store local game settings and high score on the device

## Local Storage

Block OS stores settings and high score locally on the player's device. This
local data is used only to keep the game experience consistent between sessions.

## Advertising

Block OS uses Google AdMob to show banner ads and rewarded ads. Rewarded ads are
optional and are used to grant in-game rewards such as extra hearts or removes.
AdMob may use data according to Google's advertising policies.

## Children's Privacy

Block OS is intended for a general casual gaming audience. The app does not
knowingly collect direct personal information from children. If you believe a
child has provided personal information, contact us so we can review and respond.

## Data Sharing

Block OS does not sell personal information. Data may be processed by the
third-party services listed above according to their own policies and only for
app functionality, analytics, notifications, advertising, and diagnostics.

## Your Choices

Players can disable sound, music, and vibration in the Settings screen. Ad and
analytics choices may also be available through device settings, Google account
settings, or regional consent prompts provided by Google services.
